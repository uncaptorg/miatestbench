# DVPRS — Defense and Veterans Pain Rating Scale

**Domain:** Pain  
**Population:** Adults; veteran and first responder populations; OpenArms program

## Description
11-point NRS (0–10) for pain intensity plus 4 supplementary items measuring pain interference with sleep, mood, activity, and stress. Developed for military and veteran populations with high rates of combat-related chronic pain.

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

| NRS Score | Severity | Clinical Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | No pain | Monitor |
| 1–3 | Mild pain | Self-management; analgesia as needed |
| 4–6 | Moderate pain | Multidisciplinary pain assessment; pharmacotherapy review |
| 7–10 | Severe pain | Urgent pain clinic/specialist referral |

- **Supplementary items ≥5** = high pain interference → multidisciplinary pain management programme.
- Chronic pain + PTSD is a highly prevalent comorbidity in veteran populations — treat both concurrently.
- Opioid use in context of high pain + PTSD = addiction risk monitoring required.
