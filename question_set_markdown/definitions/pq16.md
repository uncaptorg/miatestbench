# PQ-16 — Prodromal Questionnaire (16-item)

**Domain:** Psychosis-Like Experiences / Ultra-High Risk  
**Population:** Young people and adults (14+)

## Description
16-item self-report screen for attenuated psychotic symptoms associated with ultra-high risk (UHR) for psychosis. Each endorsed item followed by a distress rating. Covers unusual perceptual experiences, ideas of reference, magical thinking, and disorganised thinking.

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

| Endorsed Items | Interpretation | Clinical Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–5 | Below threshold | Monitor if family history or other risk factors |
| ≥6 | Positive screen | Referral to early psychosis service for CAARMS assessment |

- **≥6 with distress** increases specificity for true UHR status.
- Positive screen = refer for comprehensive CAARMS or SIPS interview; do not diagnose psychosis from PQ-16 alone.
- Transition to psychosis rate from UHR: ~15–35% over 2 years without intervention.
