# SHALOM — Spiritual Health and Life-Orientation Measure

**Domain:** Spiritual Health  
**Population:** Adults (16+)

## Description
20-item measure of spiritual wellbeing across 4 domains (5 items each): Personal/Intrapersonal (sense of identity, meaning, purpose), Communal/Interpersonal (love of others, forgiveness), Environmental (awe, care for nature), and Transcendental (relationship with God/Divine/Creator). Each item rated on two scales: "I feel…" and "I value…" (1–5 each).

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

Each domain has a **Lived Experience score** (I feel) and a **Formal Ideology score** (I value), both averaged 1–5.

| Pattern | Interpretation | Clinical Action |
|---|---|---|
| Feel ≈ Value (both ≥3) | Spiritual wellbeing aligned | No action; may be a resilience factor |
| Feel < Value (gap >1.5) | Spiritual incongruence | Explore unmet spiritual needs; spiritual care referral |
| Both < 2 | Low spiritual wellbeing | Assess isolation, meaning-making, and existential distress |

- **Large feel–value gap = spiritual incongruence** — associated with psychological distress and reduced quality of life.
- Transcendental domain elevation predicts resilience in chronic illness and grief.
- Not used to diagnose or pathologise — opens conversation about meaning, purpose, and connection.
