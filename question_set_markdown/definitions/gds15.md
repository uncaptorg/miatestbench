# GDS-15 — Geriatric Depression Scale (15-item)

**Domain:** Depressed Mood  
**Population:** Older adults (65+)

## Description
15-item yes/no self-report designed for older adults. Deliberately excludes somatic items (fatigue, sleep, appetite) that may reflect physical illness rather than depression, improving specificity in medically unwell older populations.

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

| Score | Severity | Clinical Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–4 | Normal | No action; monitor if risk factors present |
| 5–8 | Mild depression | Review; consider psychotherapy |
| 9–11 | Moderate depression | Treatment warranted; GP review |
| 12–15 | Severe depression | Urgent review; high likelihood of MDD |

- **≥5** warrants clinical attention; **≥10** = high probability of major depression in older adults.
- Cannot substitute for clinical interview in dementia — use with cognitive screen.
