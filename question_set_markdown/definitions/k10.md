# K10 — Kessler Psychological Distress Scale (10-item)

**Domain:** Psychological Distress  
**Population:** Adults (18+); Australian population-normed

## Description
10-item self-report measuring non-specific psychological distress (anxiety and depression symptoms) over the past 4 weeks. Scored 1–5 per item (total 10–50). Widely used as a population-level screen in Australian primary care and mental health services.

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

| Score | Likely Status | Clinical Action |
|---|---|---|
| 10–19 | Likely well | No intervention indicated; health promotion |
| 20–24 | Likely mild disorder | GP review; brief psychological intervention |
| 25–29 | Likely moderate disorder | Mental health referral; GP Mental Health Care Plan |
| 30–50 | Likely severe disorder | Urgent mental health review; specialist referral |

- **Australian cut-off ≥22** for referral to mental health services (PHN/MBS criterion).
- **≥30** highly likely to meet criteria for a high-prevalence disorder (anxiety or depression).
- Used routinely in Australian ATAPS/Better Access outcomes monitoring.
