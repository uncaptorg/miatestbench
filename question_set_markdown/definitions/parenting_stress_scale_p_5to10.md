# Parenting Stress Scale (Parent 5–10)

**Domain:** Parenting Stress  
**Population:** Parents of children 5–10

## Description
Measures parenting stress across two dimensions: Parental Distress (PD) and Parent-Child Dysfunctional Interaction (PCDI). Assesses carer burden and relationship strain.

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

- Higher score = greater parenting stress.
- **High PD** = parent mental health support needed (carer wellbeing intervention).
- **High PCDI** = parent-child relationship difficulties → parent management training or family therapy.
- Parenting stress is a key environmental risk factor for child mental health outcomes — address alongside child's presenting problems.
