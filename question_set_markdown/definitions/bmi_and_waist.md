# BMI and Waist Circumference

**Domain:** Physical Health  
**Population:** Adults and adolescents

## Description
Body Mass Index (weight kg / height m²) and waist circumference measurement. Key metabolic monitoring indicators, particularly important in clients on antipsychotics or mood stabilisers with known metabolic side effects.

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

**BMI (adults):**

| BMI | Category | Clinical Action |
|---|---|---|
| <18.5 | Underweight | Dietitian referral; eating disorder screen |
| 18.5–24.9 | Normal weight | Monitor |
| 25.0–29.9 | Overweight | Lifestyle intervention |
| ≥30.0 | Obese | Metabolic review; consider medication change |

**Waist Circumference:**
- **Men >94 cm / Women >80 cm** = increased cardiometabolic risk.
- **Men >102 cm / Women >88 cm** = substantially increased risk.

- Critical annual monitoring in all clients on antipsychotics, lithium, or valproate.
