# C-SSRS — Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale

**Domain:** Suicidality  
**Population:** All ages; clinical gold standard

## Description
Clinician-administered structured interview assessing suicidal ideation intensity and suicidal behaviour. The most widely validated suicidality measure used in clinical trials, emergency departments, and routine care globally.

## Scoring & Clinical Interpretation

**Ideation subscale (1–5):**

| Level | Description | Clinical Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Wish to be dead | Safety assessment; explore passive ideation |
| 2 | Non-specific active suicidal ideation | Safety plan; increase contact |
| 3 | Active ideation without plan | Safety plan; restrict means; increase monitoring |
| 4 | Active ideation with plan | Crisis intervention; consider inpatient |
| 5 | Active ideation with intent and plan | Emergency referral; immediate safety action |

**Behaviour subscale:** Any preparatory behaviour, interrupted or aborted attempt, or actual attempt = high risk requiring immediate action.

- Ideation duration > 1 hour/day and intensity "unable to control" = elevated risk markers.
- Recent attempt (past 3 months) is the strongest predictor of future attempt.
